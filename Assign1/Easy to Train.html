Low Maintenance Dog
Low maintenance dogs are for those who may not have time to train a stubborn breed. These dogs are also great companion for those with alternating schedules and less consistency in their lives. Like most dogs, training during their younger years makes learning much easier. Training will still take time, but once these dogs are trained, it is rare they will stray from their training.
Who are low maintenance dogs ideal for?
  • First time dog owners.
  • Anyone overwhelmed with the idea of owning a new dog.
  • Individuals who balance inconsistent schedules.
  • Those who do not necessarily have time to train a stubborn breed.
  • Those who plan to keep their dog indoors (The energy level of the breed should still be considered).

Examples of low maintenance breeds:
  • French Bulldog Known for being a playful companion. Once trained, these dogs tend to be a low maintenance breed.
  • Beagle An easy-going and outgoing friend. This dog enjoys walks, but does not require much of them.
  • Chihuahuas Known for being a bit vocal, the chihuahua is also an easy breed to train and does not require grooming.
  • Bull Mastiff These dogs may look intimmidating, based on their large size, but are a gentle and easy-going beast.
  • Rat Terrier Easy to train and loves company. This social dog requires no ngrooming and loves to learn.
