Low Energy Dog
Low energy dogs are a gentle companion for the elderly, those with physical conditions preventing them from outdoor activity. These dogs are also great for apartment and tiny home living. These dogs can range from being great company for the eldery, as well as anyone who just wants to relax with a cuddly friend after a long day. Most importantly, these dogs rarely need much activity, making them a great companion for the average homebody.

Examples of Low Energy Dogs
  • Bloodhoundsare a gentle and calm giant.
  • Chow Chow is known for being a loyal family companion.
  • Pekingese is known for being a low energy lap dog.
  • Havanese is a small but sturdy dog. This dog is best for cooler temperatures and has long hair.
Who are low energy dogs a great match for?
  • The elderly.
  • Those living in small living spaces.
  • Single person households.
  • Cold-weathered cities.
  • Those who prefer to stay home.
  • Someone in search of a lap dog.
    •
Who should avoid low energs dogs?
  • People looking for an activity partner.
  • Households with young children. (Low energy dogs tend to have less patience for playfulness).
  • Households looking for excitement and energy from a dog.
